Cloud Computing

Simply put, cloud computing is the delivery of computing services—including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence—over the internet (“the cloud”) to offer faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale.

Function of Cloud Computing

  1. Server

    2. virtual (not physical) servers running in a cloud computing environment that can be accessed on demand by unlimited users.

  2. Storage

    3. a mode of computer data storage in which digital data is stored on servers in off-site locations

  3. Data Analytics

    4. the application of analytic algorithms in the cloud against data in a private or public cloud to then deliver a result of interest.

  4. Audio and Video

    5. loud-based audio streaming enables users to access and enjoy audio content, such as music, over the internet at any time

  5. Creating and Testing apps

    6. Cloud computing offers scalability, flexibility, and cost-effectiveness for app development

Benefits

  • Cost Saving

    • Cloud computing reduces the need for expensive infrastructure and offers a pay-as-you-go model, allowing businesses to pay only for the services they use

  • Scalability

    • Organizations can easily scale their storage and computing needs up or down based on demand, providing flexibility

  • Flexibility and Collaboration

    • Cloud enables employees to work from anywhere, promoting flexibility and facilitating collaboration on projects across different locations

  • Business Continuity

    • Cloud services ensure data is safely stored and protected, allowing for quick recovery in case of outages or crises

  • Competitive Edg

    • By handling IT infrastructure maintenance and other aspects, cloud computing gives businesses a competitive advantage by minimizing time and resource investments

  • High Availability

    • Cloud services are distributed across multiple facilities, ensuring high availability and minimal downtime

  • Security

    • Cloud providers offer built-in encryption, compliance certifications, and tools to monitor security, enhancing data protection

  • Automation

    • Cloud computing allows for automation of tasks, improving efficiency and reducing the risk of errors

  • Global Reach

    • Cloud services enable worldwide deployment of applications, providing instant access and reducing latency for users globally

  • Environmental Sustainability

    • Cloud infrastructure is more environmentally friendly, reducing energy consumption, carbon footprint, and promoting sustainability

    Disdvantages

  • Downtime

    • Cloud computing systems are internet-based, making them vulnerable to service outages that can disrupt business operations and lead to financial losses

  • Security and Privacy Concerns

    • Data security and privacy threats are significant disadvantages of cloud computing, with inadequate security measures leading to data breaches, intellectual property theft, and malware attacks

  • Vulnerability to Attacks

    • Businesses using public, hybrid, or multi-cloud services are at risk of cyber attacks, potentially resulting in data loss or leakage due to the shared network infrastructure

  • Limited Control and Flexibility

    • In public, hybrid, and community clouds, cloud service providers manage all services, limiting customers' control over their data and applications. This lack of control can impact operational flexibility and customization options

  • Vendor Lock-in

    • Vendor lock-in occurs when companies using a specific cloud provider's services find it challenging to switch to another provider due to high switching costs, data migration complexities, and other constraints. This can limit flexibility and hinder operational efficiency
